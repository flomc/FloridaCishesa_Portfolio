Author: Florida Cishesa, B.S. in Mechanical Engineering 2026

1. Overview

  • This is the end product from Design and Manufacturing I, a sophomore capstone class. The robot won a match at the competition.
  • This machine takes as input human input and produces electricity and can serve to power electric devices.

2. Inspiration

I wanted to design a machine that could perform the tasks of the competitions the simplest and the fastest way possible.

3. Process and Methodology


Methodologies and tools used:
  • SolidWorks for design of parts
  • CNC milling, lathe, saws, press brake to manufacture the parts
  • Motors, batteries, receiver, and other electronic components

Process:

  • The project started with brainstorming different concepts and developing a strategy.
  • This was followed by designing my most critical module (MCM).
  • Power analysis to perform the desired tasks with the availble resources.
  • Finally, working towards a finished robot, testing and iterating.


Challenges and Learning:
  • Design takes time. Starting early and testing provide a more efficent process.
  • The rear wheels didn't have enough traction, causing slipping and skipping.
    1. A counterweight was added above the rear wheels to increase traction.

4. Results

I chose a four-stage telescoping linear slide. A gear-chain of 1:2 ratio was used to deploy the lift mechanism.

  • Two torque servos were used in this gear chain to allow both high speed and high torque.
  • Used delrin (high strength and stiffness, low friction and wear resistance) to manufacture pulleys and winches for minimal energy loss
  • Mounted a speed servo to the end of the telescoping slide, and an aluminum bar wrapped in a double-sided tape to perfom lifting and removal of tubes on the competition board.
  • Mounted the motor, and connected electric components for a successfull RC control
  • Won a match by 120 points during the competition
