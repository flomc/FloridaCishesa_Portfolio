About two thirds of the container were filled with water. Then the light of the beam was pointed so that it hit one side of the container to make the incident angle, then the light refracted at another side of the container.

Different methods were used by each group member for finding multiple angle of incidence and refraction.

4. Second run Procedure

  • About two third of the container were filled with water.
  • The ray of the laser hit the container at some angle to the normal.
  • Carefully, we took a picture using our smartphones, by making sure the plane of the camera is right about the incident point and parallel to the plane of the container.
  • We inserted the image in logger pro, then we located several points on the light ray
  • We made sure the points are far enough from each other to minimize the error.
  • We found the slope of the ray.
  • We used arctangent to find the angle.
  • We checked the correct angle.
