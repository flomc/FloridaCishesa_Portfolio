1. Introduction

  • The goal of this experiment is to test a method to find the index of refraction of water by using Snell‚Äôs law which is given by :
  • n1 sin(Œ∏1)= n2sin(Œ∏2)

2. First Run Procedure

About two thirds of the container were filled with water. Then the light of the beam was pointed so that it hit one side of the container to make the incident angle, then the light refracted at another side of the container.

Different methods were used by each group member for finding multiple angle of incidence and refraction.

4. Second run Procedure

  • About two third of the container were filled with water.
  • The ray of the laser hit the container at some angle to the normal.
  • Carefully, we took a picture using our smartphones, by making sure the plane of the camera is right about the incident point and parallel to the plane of the container.
  • We inserted the image in logger pro, then we located several points on the light ray
  • We made sure the points are far enough from each other to minimize the error.
  • We found the slope of the ray.
  • We used arctangent to find the angle.
  • We checked the correct angle.

6. Results

Images of our new system are below, as well as a diagram showing how all of the parts are connected electronically. This new system reinforces the water resistivity of the system, is more robust and easier to assemble, and is self-sustained through the solar panel and external portable charger eliminating the need for battery replacement mid recording periods.

7. Impact

  • There is no need for multiple trips to the site during a collection period of data
  • There will be no unexpected interruption in the collection of data
  • The equipment is more secure with the attachments
  • Easy assembly and disassembly
  • Solar recharge to provide autonomy of the system

8. Future Work

Future improvements and work could include the following:

  • Add more solar panels for quicker recharge of the battery
  • Add a stronger portable charger with more than 5V of power
  • Add more durability to the system
  • Redesigning the reliance of the system on the rebar

9. Conclusion

Overall, we were able to design a new data logger system for the Blackfeet Indian Reservation. Our new design is more robust, allows for easy assembly, and eliminates the need for battery replacement throughout a data collection period. Overall, this project was a great experience to teach the importance of iteration and prioritization when it comes to prototyping. Future ideas we have for this project are to enable the data logger with a system to alert the user when there is need for maintenance with any of the parts and to expand the capabilities of the system to allow for a remote transfer of data.
