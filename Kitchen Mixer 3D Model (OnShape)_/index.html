3D Model: Manual Stand Mixer

Acknowledgements

Author: Florida Cishesa, B.S. in Mechanical Engineering 2026

1. Overview

  • This is the final project from Fall 2020 Engineering Graphics
  • The task was to solid model any object of our choice that would meet certain criteria.
  • I was tasked to create an assembly in OnShape composed of at most 9 parts.
  • Relative motions between parts had requirements such as revolute mate and pin slot mate.
  • The final result had to include a detailed engineering drawing of the most complex part in the assembly.
  • The project had to reflect all the topics we learned throughout the quater, such as good dimensioning, tolerancing, and drawing .

2. Inspiration

When presented with the final project instructions, I was driven not only to meet the minimum requirements but also to embark on a learning journey. I began by reflecting on my hobbies and instances where I fell short due to a lack of resources. Baking has always been a passion of mine. However, I often found myself laboriously mixing ingredients with a manual whisk, a task that an electric stand mixer could accomplish with ease. This sparked curiosity - could I create a manual stand mixer that could rival its electric counterpart in efficiency? This question ignited my creativity and became the foundation of my final project.

3. Process and Methodology


Methodologies and tools used:
  • CAD OnShape for design of parts and the assembly

Process:

  • The project started with brainstorming a design that would meet all the requirements for a successful project.
  • This was followed by breaking down the different interations between parts and thinking about the structure of each part to achieve the desired motion.
  • The next step was starting designing each part, and assembling them.
  • Finally, it was time for testing, and refining the prototype


Challenges and Learning:
  • I was constrained to 9 parts in total that could achieve all the requirements
    1. I had to think about minimize my structure of the assembly, while cretaing a product that can still work.
  • Keeping thrack of all the parts and interactions they have among them was necessary
    1. I achieved part management by naming each action with a descriptive name such that I could easily differentiate them.
