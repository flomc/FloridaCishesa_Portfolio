1. Overview

  • This is the final project from Fall 2020 Engineering Graphics
  • The task was to solid model any object of our choice that would meet certain criteria.

2. Inspiration

To meet

  • With the currect energy crisis, we wanted to design a system that can produce clean energy with a low cost mechanical input that could be easily implemented anywhere.
  • This can specially help developping countries provide electricity to their population even in the most remote areas.

3. Project Goals

The main objectives of this projects were to:

  • 1. Develop an electric generator that can highly amplify a low mechanical input
  • 2. Leave flexibility to the source of input to help tailor the system to any environment
  • 3. Keep the design relatively compact with cheap but duarble material

4. Process and Methodology


Methodologies and tools used:
  • SolidWorks for design of parts
  • Original Prusa i3 3D printer

Process:

  • The project started with brainstorming the transmission system.
  • This was followed by designing the circuit and the gears in SolidWorks
  • After printing, testing, and refining the prototype, the final device was assembled.


Challenges and Learning:
  • We needed the system to stay compact and easy to assemble
    1. We solved this problem by defining a cofiguration that would minimize the distance between components. In addition, we computed the gear ratio that would achieve the same output with gears that can fit the new configuration.
  • We needed to figure out the best oriantation of the rotor relative to the input, in this case, a hand turning a hinge.
    1. We made the central plate stand vertical with two opposetly oriented attachemnt to keep it balanced when the rotor is rotating.

5. Results and Impact

Final Set Up:

  • - Six permanent magnets symmetrically positioned next to each other and constituting the rotor
  • - Four coils symmetrically aligned with the rotor
  • - A central plate where the rotor and the magnets are fermely attached
  • - Four gears 3D printed, two by two identical. One of the smaller gears hanging on the same shaft as the rotor
    •

    The electric generator successfully amplifies the input to product current. This is of 1:30 ratio between the input gear and the rotor. This device can now provide its user electric current anywhere they are without requiring too much mechanical force for input.

    6. Future Work

    Plans for the future include:

    • Scalling up the device to produce enough current to power bigger devices
    • Programmig the device to produce the desirable current and volatge
    • Converting any input into an oscillating signal before it is transmitted to the rotor
